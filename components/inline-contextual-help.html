About Safety Plans

A safety plan helps you identify:

  • Warning signs that you might be struggling
  • Coping strategies that work for you
  • People and places that help you feel safe
  • Ways to make your environment safer

There's no right or wrong way to create one. This is your personal tool.

Understanding Your Assessment

Your responses help us understand your current well-being. This assessment uses questions tested with thousands of people (a ) to provide the best support.

What does "clinically validated" mean?

It means the questions have been carefully tested with thousands of people to ensure they accurately measure what they're designed to measure. Healthcare professionals worldwide trust these tools.

Your answers help create a clear picture, but they're just one part of understanding you. Your experiences and feelings matter most.

HIPAA Protection

HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) is a federal law that keeps your health information private and secure.

What this means for you:

  • Your information stays confidential
  • We can't share it without your written permission
  • You control who sees your records
  • Your data is encrypted and protected

Listing Your Medications

Please list all medications you're currently taking, including:

  • Prescription medications
  • Over-the-counter medications
  • Vitamins and supplements
  • Herbal remedies

Include the dosage if you know it (e.g., "50mg"). If you're not sure, that's okay - your provider will help clarify.

Accessibility Features

  • Keyboard Navigation: All triggers are keyboard accessible (Tab, Enter, Escape)
  • Screen Reader Support: Proper ARIA labels, roles, and state management
  • Focus Management: Focus returns to trigger on close
  • Reduced Motion: Respects prefers-reduced-motion settings
  • Color Contrast: WCAG AA compliant color ratios
  • Touch Targets: Minimum 44x44px for mobile accessibility
  • Smart Positioning: Content adjusts to viewport boundaries

Usage Guidelines

  • Tooltip: Use for brief clarifications (1-2 sentences)
  • Popover: Use for detailed explanations with lists or multiple paragraphs
  • Inline Expandable: Use when help should flow naturally in content
  • Avoid Overuse: Too many help triggers can overwhelm users
  • Clear Labeling: Ensure context makes the trigger's purpose obvious
